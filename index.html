Scope & Motivation

Call for Papers

The convergence of artificial intelligence and energy systems is reshaping how smart grids are designed, operated, and controlled. Agentic AI — encompassing autonomous agents, multi-agent systems, and large language model-driven orchestration — holds transformative promise for energy management, demand response, distributed generation, and grid resilience. Yet deploying such systems at scale introduces new challenges around safety, interpretability, coordination, and real-time decision-making under uncertainty.

The AEGIS Workshop (Agentic Energy Systems in Smart Grids) invites original research contributions that advance the theoretical foundations, enabling technologies, and practical applications of agentic AI in modern power and energy systems. We welcome work spanning algorithm design, system architecture, simulation, and experimental validation that pushes the frontier of intelligent, autonomous energy infrastructure.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

Agentic AI frameworks and architectures for energy management systems
Multi-agent reinforcement learning for smart grid control and optimization
Large language models (LLMs) for autonomous grid operation and decision support
Distributed and federated learning in energy networks
Demand response and flexibility management with autonomous agents
Peer-to-peer energy trading enabled by multi-agent systems
Agentic control of microgrids and virtual power plants
Integration of EVs and distributed energy resources via autonomous coordination
Safety, reliability, and interpretability of AI in critical grid infrastructure
Digital twins and simulation environments for agentic energy systems
Human-agent interaction in energy management and control rooms
Communication protocols and data exchange for autonomous energy agents
Cybersecurity and adversarial robustness of agentic energy systems
Real-world deployments, testbeds, and case studies
Evaluation metrics and benchmarks for agentic smart grid systems

How to Submit

All papers must be submitted electronically via EDAS. Submissions should follow the standard IEEE conference paper format (double-column, up to 6 pages including references). Papers should present original, unpublished work not currently under review elsewhere.

Full submission instructions and the official portal link will be available at the IEEE SmartGridComm 2026 conference website. Please check the conference website for the most up-to-date guidelines.
